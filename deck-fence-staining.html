Deck staining in Sayreville, Woodbridge, and Carteret

Backyard decks in Sayreville, Woodbridge, and Carteret take a beating from sun and rain. Sal schedules wash days and coat days around the forecast — stain applied too early or before rain fails within a season.

Transparent vs solid stain

Transparent and semi-transparent stains show grain but need more frequent refresh. Solid stain or deck paint hides gray wood and lasts longer on horizontal surfaces. Sal recommends based on wood age and your maintenance preference.

Fence lines and neighbor sides

Side-by-side fences may need coordination with neighbors for access. Sal quotes reachable surfaces and notes if both sides are included. See fence examples in the project gallery.

Railings and vertical surfaces

Vertical pickets and railings often outlast horizontal deck boards. Sal may recommend different products for foot traffic vs. vertical wood on the same project. Pair deck work with exterior trim painting when porch columns and railings need paint instead of stain.

How deck & fence staining jobs run

  1. Wash and dry — Mildew removal, dry window before stain.
  2. Prep gray wood — Sand or brighten as needed for adhesion.
  3. Coat and cure — Brush/roll stain, keep off surface until cured.
