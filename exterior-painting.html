Season and scheduling

Exterior work runs spring through fall when temps and humidity allow proper cure times. Sal schedules around weather windows and communicates if a stretch of rain pushes a phase.

Surface prep before the first coat

Loose paint is scraped, mildew is washed, gaps are caulked, and bare wood gets primer. Metal railings near the bay may need rust-inhibiting primer. Sal won't coat over active chalking without addressing adhesion first.

Curb appeal before you list

Pre-listing exterior refreshes are common in Woodbridge and Edison — front elevation, door, and trim often make the biggest impact for buyers driving by. Pair with deck or fence staining for backyard showing appeal. See exterior painting ballparks or browse exterior project photos.

Exterior trim, shutters, and doors

Focused curb-appeal packages often combine siding with front door and shutter painting — a faster scope than a full house when the body color is still sound. Sal phases work so the street-facing elevation is done first when your timeline is tight.
