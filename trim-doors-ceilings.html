The details that define a finished room

Walls get the attention, but trim and ceilings frame the space. Sal's Painting sands and deglosses old trim, caulks gaps, paints doors off hinges when needed, and cuts clean lines where wall meets ceiling.

Trim and ceiling work

  • Baseboards, chair rail, and crown moulding
  • Interior doors — slab and panel styles
  • Window and door casings
  • Ceiling repaints — flat white and color
  • Handrails and built-in shelving
Yellowed trim: Oil-based trim from the 1990s often yellows over time. Sal may recommend a deglosser and waterborne enamel for a whiter, harder finish that holds up to cleaning.

Trim painting in Woodbridge, Iselin, and Edison

Colonials and split-levels in Woodbridge, Iselin, and Edison often need baseboard and casing refreshes when walls get new color. Sal coordinates trim sheen with your interior wall paint so the room reads finished, not half-done.

Spray vs brush on doors

Panel doors with detail often get brush-and-roll; flat slab doors can be sprayed in a controlled setup for a factory-smooth finish. Sal picks the method that matches your doors and timeline.

Ceiling-only repaints

After smoke, leaks, or yellowing, ceilings can be refreshed without touching walls. Walls are masked, edges are cut cleanly, and flat or matte finishes hide roller texture on overhead surfaces.

Two-tone and trim contrast schemes

White trim against colored walls — or against an accent wall — is the classic Middlesex County look. Sal caulks the joint first so the contrast line stays crisp after the job cures. Patchy walls get drywall prep before trim is cut in.

Exterior-facing doors and shutters

Entry doors and shutters pair with exterior painting scopes when curb appeal is the goal. Sal quotes door prep separately from whole-house exterior when you only need the front refreshed.

How trim, doors & ceilings jobs run

  1. Sheen and color plan — Trim, doors, ceilings defined before materials ordered.
  2. Prep and degloss — Sand, caulk, prime stained or glossy trim as needed.
  3. Sharp cut-in — Brush work at edges, roll fields, rehang doors cleanly.
